Ke Zeng — health economist studying healthcare reform and alternative payment models, with a focus on how providers and the private sector respond to policy innovation.

My job market paper investigates the effects of the Comprehensive Care for Joint Replacement (CJR) Model on both Medicare and commercially insured patients, documenting near dollar-to-dollar cost spillovers across payer types and practice-style changes in discharge decisions.

I also work on causal inference methodology, including comparisons between synthetic control and matching approaches for counterfactual construction.
